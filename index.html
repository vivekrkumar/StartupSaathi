The Problem - Navigating the Startup Maze

Indian startups face a multitude of complexities beyond just building a product.

  • Bureaucratic Hurdles: Complex and time-consuming registration processes across various government bodies.
  • Compliance Overload: Constant deadlines for GST, IT, PF, and other regulatory filings, leading to penalties if missed.
  • Funding Gap & Dilution Fears: Difficulty identifying the right investors and understanding complex capital raise structures and equity dilution.
  • Lack of Centralized Guidance: No single platform for all essential non-core business needs, from ideation to funding.

Funding & Compliance Hub

Connecting you to capital and keeping you compliant.

1. Intelligent Funding Matchmaking:

  • Matching startups with relevant investors (Govt. funds, State Govt. schemes, Incubators, Angels, VCs) based on:
    • Nature of Startup (Sector, Innovation)
    • Stage (Seed, Early, Growth)
    • Funding Needs & Criteria
  • Key Indian Funding Options:
    • Government Schemes:
      • Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS): Up to ₹20 Lakhs as grant for PoC/prototype; up to ₹50 Lakhs investment via convertible debentures/debt for market entry/scaling. Disbursed through incubators.
      • Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS): Loan guarantees up to ₹5 crore from banks, with up to 80% guarantee.
      • Atal Innovation Mission (AIM): Grants up to ₹10 crore for innovative solutions in healthcare, education, agriculture. Includes support for Atal Tinkering Labs (ATLs) and Atal Incubation Centers (AICs).
      • Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS): ₹10,000 crore corpus managed by SIDBI, investing in SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) which then invest in startups.
      • Stand-Up India Scheme: Facilitates bank loans from ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore for SC/ST and women entrepreneurs for greenfield projects.
      • NIDHI Programme (National Initiative for Developing and Harnessing Innovations): Various components (e.g., NIDHI-SSS, NIDHI-PRAYAS) provide grants and support via Technology Business Incubators (TBIs) across different stages of innovation.
    • State Government Policies: Many states (e.g., Karnataka, Assam, Gujarat) have their own startup policies offering grants, matching funds, and infrastructure support to incubators and startups.
    • Incubators & Accelerators: Over 979 incubators & accelerators in India provide seed funding (e.g., Axilor Ventures: $500k-$750k per startup), mentorship, and networking (e.g., Indian Angel Network, Venture Catalysts, India Accelerator).
    • Angel Investors & Networks: High-net-worth individuals and networks (e.g., Indian Angel Network, LetsVenture, Mumbai Angels) providing early-stage capital (typically $100k-$1M) and mentorship.
    • Venture Capital (VC) Firms: Focus on early to growth-stage investments (Series A onwards). Diverse focus sectors including FinTech, SaaS, HealthTech, AI, Consumer Tech (e.g., Sequoia India, Blume Ventures, Elevation Capital).
  • Capital Raise Calculators: Tools for Equity, Convertible Debentures, and Dilution analysis.
